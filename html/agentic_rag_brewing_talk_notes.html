AI, Brewing Knowledge, and Competition-Ready Process

Talk notes for a National Homebrew Competition presentation

AI did not replace brewing judgment. It became useful when it was forced to respect brewing judgment.
AI did not replace brewing judgment Grounded process beats chat novelty Books + records + validation changed the output

Core Framing

AI alone can help. AI plus brewing knowledge helps more. AI plus curated brewing knowledge, process control, and real batch feedback is where it becomes genuinely useful for competition brewing.

Chat AI gives ideas. A grounded brewing system gives repeatable decisions, better records, and more defensible recipes.

Without The Books

My recipes would still be better than average homebrew AI output, but materially worse than they are now.

  • still usable
  • still able to make beer
  • more drift-prone
  • less style-grounded
  • less defensible when making exact choices

What The Books Changed

  • They gave real style anchors instead of vibe-based guesses.
  • They improved clone fidelity.
  • They tightened process decisions like yeast handling, fermentation structure, and bitterness shaping.
  • They made the system more corrective, not just generative.

The Real Difference

This is not bad recipes versus good recipes.

  • Without the books: decent but drift-prone recipes.
  • With the books and the system: grounded, style-aware, defensible recipes that are much more suitable for competition refinement.

What Makes This Different From Normal AI Use

Most brewers who have tried AI have used it for chat, quick lookups, or brainstorming. That is not what this system does.

  • The recipe lives in the repo.
  • The brew-day sheet lives in the repo.
  • The inventory lives in the repo.
  • The batch history lives in the repo.
  • The shopping intent lives in the repo.
  • The guardrails and validations live in the repo.

So the system is not “remembering” by magic. It is writing durable brewing state into a controlled apparatus.

Simple Ladder

  • Chat AI: gives ideas
  • RAG: gives context
  • Repo-RAG: works against real files
  • Agentic Repo-RAG: can operate the workflow and reduce drift

Why Brewing Benefits

  • Brewing is iterative.
  • Brewing has drift everywhere.
  • Process notes matter.
  • Ingredient inventory matters.
  • Batch feedback matters.
  • Style references matter.

What The System Actually Does

  • Creates and syncs recipe artifacts.
  • Generates brew-day documents and print views.
  • Tracks inventory and shopping intent.
  • Registers brews and packaging events.
  • Preserves what I did, when I did it, and how I did it.
  • Validates drift so new changes do not quietly break the apparatus.
  • Makes it easier to keep competition-ready versions distinct from historical drafts.

Good Presentation Line

Without the books, it can still make beer. With the books and this system, it can make beers that are much more likely to be accurate, repeatable, and competition-capable.

Another Good Line

The win is not that AI writes recipes. The win is that the brewing process becomes more disciplined, more traceable, and easier to improve.

Suggested Talk Flow

  1. Start with what most people think AI in brewing is: chat, ideas, quick suggestions.
  2. Show why that is not enough for serious recipe development or competition prep.
  3. Explain what changed when books, inventory, brew logs, and guardrails became part of the system.
  4. Show that the value is not one smart answer, but consistent operational follow-through.
  5. End on the idea that brewing is a strong domain for this kind of system because it is iterative, stateful, and process-sensitive.

Speaker Notes

Do not oversell the AI. For this audience, the stronger argument is that the system got better because it was grounded.

Brewing books matter. Batch records matter. Inventory truth matters. Repeatable workflow matters. Real brew feedback matters.

If you want one line to keep coming back to, use this: AI did not replace brewing judgment. It became useful when it was forced to respect brewing judgment.
