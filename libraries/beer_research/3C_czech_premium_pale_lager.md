# Czech Premium Pale Lager (BJCP 3C) — Style Doctrine

## Core Philosophy
- This is a pale lager with clear Czech identity: rounded malt depth plus firm, noble bitterness.
- Bitterness should be assertive but never rough.
- Drinkability comes from integration, not minimal intensity.
- The finish should be long and balanced, not clipped-dry or sweet-heavy.

## Medal-Oriented Identity (What Judges Reward)
- Rich, elegant bready malt character with high clarity.
- Pronounced noble hop flavor and bitterness that remains smooth.
- Clean fermentation with very low ester expression.
- No obvious diacetyl or process-derived roughness.
- Mature, polished profile from adequate cold conditioning.

## Parameter Doctrine (Competition-Safe Center)
- OG target: 1.048-1.054
- FG target: 1.013-1.015
- ABV target: 4.8-5.4%
- IBU target: 36-42
- SRM target: 4-5
- Carbonation target: 2.4-2.6 vols

These center points stay clearly in 3C while preserving balance and judge appeal.

## Grain Doctrine
Base:
- 85-100% high-quality Czech/Bohemian Pils malt.

Optional support:
- 3-10% light Munich can add bready depth when needed.

Avoid:
- Significant crystal/caramel additions.
- Adjunct-heavy neutralization of malt character.
- Dark malts that shift color/flavor outside 3C.

## Hop Doctrine
Profile:
- Noble hop expression (Saaz-led) with floral/spicy character.

IBU structure:
- Most bitterness from 60 min addition.
- Layer flavor with restrained mid/late additions.
- Keep whirlpool soft and noble; avoid modern aromatic signatures.

Practical pattern (5 gal / 19 L):
- 60 min bittering anchor
- 30-20 min flavor support
- 10 min plus small whirlpool for top-note lift

## Yeast Doctrine
Preferred:
- Czech lager strain for authentic profile.

House-compatible fallback:
- Clean German lager strain (34/70-type), with process tuned to retain Czech-like fullness.

Fermentation priorities:
- Adequate pitch rate for lager health.
- Cool, stable primary.
- Explicit cleanup window for VDK.
- Package only after stable gravity and clean warm-sample check.

## Water Doctrine
Goal:
- Soft-rounded bitterness, not mineral sharpness.

Starting RO build target:
- Ca: 35-55 ppm
- Mg: 3-8 ppm
- Na: 0-15 ppm
- Cl: 40-60 ppm
- SO4: 40-70 ppm
- HCO3: low

Mash pH:
- 5.30-5.40 (room-temp reading)

Avoid:
- High sulfate profiles that harden bitterness.
- Over-alkaline mashes that dull crispness and increase harshness risk.

## Mash and Brewhouse Doctrine
Mash:
- Single infusion can work.
- Step mash often improves depth and attenuation control for competition entries.

Competition-safe step profile:
1. 144.0F (62.2C) for 20 min
2. 152.0F (66.7C) for 35-45 min
3. 168.0F (75.6C) mash out for 10 min

Boil:
- 60 min strong rolling boil.
- Tight timing on hop additions to preserve bitterness quality.

## Fermentation and Maturation Doctrine
Primary:
- Pitch cold and hold around 50.0F (10.0C) through most active fermentation.

Cleanup:
- Raise to upper-50s F (14-16C) near terminal for VDK cleanup.
- Use forced VDK gate before crash.

Crash and lager:
- Crash to near-freezing after cleanup and stable FG.
- Lager at 34.0-36.0F (1.1-2.2C) for multiple weeks.

## Packaging and Service Doctrine
- Closed transfer strongly preferred.
- Carbonation in the mid style range (2.4-2.6 vols) for lift without sharpness.
- Serve cool but not icy to preserve malt/hop detail.

## Frequent Non-Professional Errors
- Treating the style like a German pils clone and losing Czech malt roundness.
- Excess sulfate that makes bitterness coarse.
- Rushed packaging before full cleanup/conditioning.
- Too little lagering time for integration and brilliance.
- Letting modern hop character intrude on noble profile.

## Diagnostic Tasting Grid (Fast Self-Scoring)
- Aroma: noble floral/spicy, clean, light bready malt
- Flavor: rich pils malt + firm smooth bitterness, long balanced finish
- Mouthfeel: medium body, medium-high carbonation, no harsh edge
- Fault check: no obvious butter, no green sulfur bite, no oxidation

If two or more fail, do not enter yet; condition longer or rebrew with process corrections.
