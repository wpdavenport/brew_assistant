# BJCP 2021 Overlay — 9C Baltic Porter

Purpose:
- Constrain recipe/process choices to BJCP 2021 9C expectations.
- Highlight judge-facing boundary risks and common deductions.
- This file is NOT style doctrine. Use with matching Baltic Porter doctrine/process intent.

## Parameter Guardrails (use as constraints)
OG: 1.060-1.090
FG: 1.016-1.024
IBU: 20-40
SRM: 17-30
ABV: 6.5-9.5%

## Sensory Intensity Targets (relative)
- Malt: High
- Roast: Low to Medium-low
- Bitterness: Medium
- Hop flavor/aroma: Low
- Ester: Low to none
- Lager character: Clean, smooth
- Carbonation: Medium

## Style Boundary Traps (judge risk)
- Roast intensity drifting into robust porter or stout
- Hot or sharp alcohol
- Sweet, syrupy finish from under-attenuation
- Too much late-hop flavor or modern hop character
- Thin body from over-drying a big dark lager
- Salty/mineral palate from overdone dark-water correction

## Common Deductions & Prevention Focus
1) Too much roast
   - Keep dark malt structural, not dominant
   - Favor smooth dark-malt choices and restrained percentages

2) Sweet, heavy finish
   - Build fermentability into the base recipe
   - Pitch enough healthy lager yeast and verify cleanup

3) Alcohol roughness
   - Control fermentation aggressively in early primary
   - Avoid large thermal swings and weak pitch rates

4) Hop mismatch
   - Keep late hopping low and traditional
   - Let bitterness support, not lead

5) Dark-water imbalance
   - Use alkalinity only as needed for pH and smoothness
   - Watch sodium levels when baking soda is involved

## Competition-Focused Process Flags
- Big lagers need more yeast than most homebrew defaults provide.
- A beer at the top of the style range can still medal, but only if the finish stays smooth and integrated.
- Over-chilling before judging hides the malt complexity that makes 9C score.
- If roast or bitterness reads angular, check water and dark-malt structure before adding sweetness next time.
