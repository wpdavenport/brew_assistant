# BJCP 2021 Overlay — 11C Strong Bitter (English Pale Ale / ESB)

Purpose:
- Constrain recipe/process choices to BJCP 2021 11C expectations.
- Highlight judge-facing boundary risks and common deductions.
- This file is NOT style doctrine. Use with matching English bitter doctrine/process intent.

## Parameter Guardrails (use as constraints)
OG: 1.048–1.060
FG: 1.010–1.016
IBU: 30–50
SRM: 8–18
ABV: 4.6–6.2%

## Sensory Intensity Targets (relative)
- Malt: Medium to Medium-high (bready/toasty/caramel support)
- Bitterness: Medium-high (firm but smooth, not harsh)
- Hop flavor/aroma: Medium to Medium-high (usually English character)
- Ester: Medium-low to Medium-high (fruit esters acceptable)
- Diacetyl: Very low to none (low is acceptable; obvious butter is a fault)
- Carbonation: Medium to Medium-high

## Style Boundary Traps (judge risk)
- Over-caramelized / sweet finish that dulls bitterness
- Harsh bitterness (water/mineral imbalance or rough hopping)
- American-citrus hop profile dominating English balance
- Too clean/neutral fermentation that strips English character
- Excess diacetyl (butter/butterscotch) beyond low background
- Roast/dark malt character drifting toward porter territory
- Oxidation: stale toffee/cardboard masking fresh bitter character

## Common Deductions & Prevention Focus
1) Sweet or heavy finish
   - Keep attenuation in range and avoid overloading crystal malts
   - Maintain a dry-enough finish to support drinkability

2) Harsh or rough bitterness
   - Prioritize smooth bittering and balanced sulfate/chloride
   - Keep late-hop choices aligned to English profile

3) Out-of-style hop character
   - Limit dominant modern tropical/dank notes for classic 11C entries
   - Build layered bitterness/flavor instead of dry-hop saturation

4) Fermentation mismatch
   - Use expressive English yeast with controlled temperature
   - Avoid both under-expression (too clean) and fault-level esters

5) Diacetyl errors
   - Include an appropriate maturation/cleanup phase
   - Verify no obvious butter before packaging/entry

## Competition-Focused Process Flags
- FG above ~1.014 at moderate OG often reads as too sweet/heavy.
- Aggressive dry hopping can push 11C toward modern pale/IPA territory.
- Very high sulfate can read sharp/mineral rather than firm English bitter.
- Early hot fermentation increases ester/fusel risk and muddies balance.
- If entered as "ESB," keep profile clearly in 11C balance rather than an IPA-like interpretation.
