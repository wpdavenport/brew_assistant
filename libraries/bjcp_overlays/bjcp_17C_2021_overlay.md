# BJCP 2021 Overlay — 17C Wee Heavy

Purpose:
- Constrain recipe/process choices to BJCP 2021 17C expectations.
- Highlight judge-facing boundary risks and common deductions.
- This file is NOT style doctrine. Use with the matching Wee Heavy doctrine/process intent.

## Parameter Guardrails (use as constraints)
OG: 1.070-1.130
FG: 1.018-1.040
IBU: 17-35
SRM: 14-25
ABV: 6.5-10.0%

## Sensory Intensity Targets (relative)
- Malt: High to very high
- Sweetness: Medium-high, but not syrupy
- Bitterness: Low to medium, just enough to balance
- Hop flavor/aroma: Low to none
- Ester: Low to moderate
- Roast: Very low, if present at all
- Alcohol: Medium-low to medium warming, never harsh
- Carbonation: Low to medium

## Style Boundary Traps (judge risk)
- Excessive roast drifting into porter/stout territory
- Modern hop flavor or aroma reading more like American strong ale
- Thin, too-dry finish that strips away the malt richness
- Syrupy sweetness from under-attenuation or overloaded crystal
- Hot, sharp alcohol
- Muddy dark-fruit/caramel stacking with no structure

## Common Deductions & Prevention Focus
1) Too sweet / cloying
   - Keep bitterness high enough to shape the palate
   - Avoid unnecessary dextrin and excessive crystal stacking

2) Too roasty
   - Use dark grains only for color correction or a faint bitter edge
   - Keep coffee/chocolate notes below stout-like intensity

3) Too much hop presence
   - Minimize late hops
   - Keep hop identity background-only

4) Hot alcohol / fermentation roughness
   - Start fermentation cool and control the rise
   - Give the beer sufficient cleanup and conditioning time

5) Thin or overly dry
   - Preserve body with mash and grist choices
   - Do not force attenuation to the point that the beer loses its signature richness

## Competition-Focused Process Flags
- Longer boil depth is acceptable, but judges care about the result, not the mythology.
- Finished beer should feel rich and smooth, not sticky or burnt.
- If the beer reads like a hoppy Scotch ale or an under-hopped old ale, expect deductions.
- Crystal complexity should resolve into a coherent malt profile, not separate sweet layers.
