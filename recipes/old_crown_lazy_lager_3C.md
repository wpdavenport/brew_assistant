# Old Crown Lazy Lager (Competition Lock)

## INTENT
Competition-locked `Old Crown Lazy Lager` built to honor Old Crown Brewing's Czech pilsner while giving the beer a real BOS path on the verified G40 + GF30 setup.

This is not documented as a direct clone.

## COMPETITION ENTRY
- BJCP Category: 3C Czech Premium Pale Lager
- Concept: hometown-inspired Czech pilsner
- Direct clone claim: no

## SOURCE BASELINE
- Historical trial archived in: `recipes/historical/old_crown_lazy_lager_trial_3C.md`
- Inspiration: Old Crown Brewing, Fort Wayne, Indiana

## TARGET PARAMETERS
- Batch size (into fermenter): 5.0 gal (19.0 L)
- OG: 1.052-1.053
- FG: 1.012-1.014
- ABV: 5.0-5.4%
- IBU: 32-35
- SRM: 3.8-4.3
- Carbonation: 2.4-2.6 vols
- Mash pH (room temp): 5.30-5.40

## FERMENTABLES
- Bohemian Pilsner Malt: 9.40 lb (4.26 kg)
- Munich II: 0.50 lb (0.23 kg)

## HOPS
Use fresh Saaz only, target lot AA ~3.5-4.5%.

- First Wort: Saaz 1.40 oz (40 g)
- 20 min: Saaz 1.10 oz (31 g)
- Whirlpool at 170.0F (76.7C) for 10 min: Saaz 0.80 oz (23 g)

### Kettle Additions
- Whirlfloc: 1 tablet at 15 min

## YEAST
Primary BOS path:
- Jasper Yeast `JY001 Czech Lager 1`, fresh and healthy, preferred

Fallback:
- A true Czech lager strain in the WY2278 / WLP802 class

Pitch target:
- ~350B cells for 5.0 gal (19.0 L) at OG 1.052-1.053

## WATER (RO BUILD)
Target profile (ppm):
- Ca: 44-46
- Mg: 2-4
- Na: 0-10
- Cl: 50-55
- SO4: 42-48
- HCO3: 0-25

For total brewing liquor ~9.45 gal (35.8 L), starting from RO:
- Gypsum (CaSO4): 0.08 oz (2.3 g)
- Calcium Chloride (CaCl2): 0.13 oz (3.8 g)
- Epsom Salt (MgSO4): 0.03 oz (0.8 g)
- Baking Soda: 0.00 oz

Phosphoric acid guidance:
- Start with 2.0 mL of 85% phosphoric acid after mash-in, not as blind pre-acidification
- Check mash pH at 10-15 min
- If still high, add 0.5-1.0 mL more, stir, wait 5 min, recheck
- Expected total range is roughly 1.5-3.0 mL

## VOLUME PLAN (G40 CALIBRATED)
Using equipment profile boil-off 1.76 gal/hr with 90 min boil:
- Post-boil target (pre-chill): ~5.7 gal (21.6 L)
- Pre-boil target: ~8.3 gal (31.6 L)
- Total liquor target: ~9.45 gal (35.8 L)

## MASH PROGRAM
Competition-safe Hochkurz:
1. 144.0F (62.2C) for 25 min
2. 152.0F (66.7C) for 40 min
3. 168.0F (75.6C) for 10 min mash out

## BOIL
- Duration: 90 min
- Add first wort Saaz before runoff begins
- Run the remaining hop schedule exactly as listed
- Whirlpool Saaz at 170.0F for 10 min only

## FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. Chill to 48.0-50.0F (8.9-10.0C), oxygenate once at pitch to ~8-10 ppm dissolved O2.
2. Hold 50.0F (10.0C) through active primary until ~50-60% apparent attenuation, not by fixed day count.
3. Raise to 58.0-60.0F (14.4-15.6C) and hold until terminal gravity is stable for 2 days.
4. Run warm forced VDK check before crash.
5. Crash to 42.0F (5.6C) after clean VDK.
6. Lager at 42.0F (5.6C) for 3-4 weeks minimum on the verified GF30 floor.

## PACKAGING + COMPETITION GATE
- Closed transfer only.
- Carbonation target: 2.4-2.6 vols.
- Do not enter unless:
  - bitterness is firm but rounded
  - malt is bready and elegant, not generic
  - no obvious butter or green-lager edge remains
  - clarity is bright and stable

## WHY THIS VERSION SHOULD WIN
- Water no longer fights the style.
- All-Saaz identity stays intact, but bitterness should read smoother.
- Mash is easier to repeat and less dependent on old process quirks.
- Czech lager fermentation remains authentic, but the cleanup gate is clearer.
- The beer should now read like a polished Czech pils, not a hard mineral trial.

## CRITICAL RISKS TO AVOID
- Using old low-AA Saaz and compensating with excess hop weight.
- Reintroducing a sulfate-heavy water profile.
- Underpitching or guessing at Jasper cell count.
- Packaging before VDK and lager maturation are fully complete.
