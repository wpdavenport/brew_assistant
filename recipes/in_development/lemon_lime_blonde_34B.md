# Lemon-Lime Blonde (First-Pass Draft)

## INTENT
First-pass concept beer built around Jasper's thiolized yeast behavior with `Cascade` first wort hopping to drive a lemon-forward expression, then supported with a restrained lime layer.

This is not meant to taste like literal soda.

The target is a dry, crisp, highly drinkable lemon-lime blonde ale that drinks bright and refreshing rather than sweet or artificial.

## COMPETITION ENTRY
- BJCP Category: 34B Mixed-Style Beer
- Base beer: light blonde ale
- Concept: lemon-lime crusher / soda-adjacent blonde

## SOURCE IDEA
- Jasper observation: thiolized yeast + `Cascade` first wort hopping can push lemon character.
- Design goal: leverage that yeast/hop interaction first, then add restrained lime support.

## TARGET PARAMETERS
- Batch size (into fermenter): 5.0 gal (19.0 L)
- OG: 1.046
- FG: 1.008
- ABV: 5.0%
- IBU: 16
- SRM: 3
- Carbonation: 2.6-2.8 vols
- Mash pH (room temp): 5.25-5.35

## FERMENTABLES
- 8.5 lb (3.86 kg) Pale Malt
- 0.5 lb (0.23 kg) Pilsner Malt
- 0.5 lb (0.23 kg) Corn Sugar

Notes:
- Keep the malt nearly invisible.
- The small sugar addition is there to dry the finish and keep the beer from reading soft or sweet.

## HOPS
Target: restrained bitterness, bright citrus support, no tropical creep.

### Boil / Whirlpool
- 0.75 oz (21 g) Cascade (6.0%) - First Wort
- 0.50 oz (14 g) Cascade (6.0%) - 20 min
- 0.75 oz (21 g) Cascade (6.0%) - 0 min at 170 F for 10 min

### Kettle Additions
- 1 tablet Whirlfloc - 15 min

## YEAST
- Jasper thiolized citrus-forward house strain (confirm exact product / culture ID before lock)

## CITRUS PLAN
- Lime zest from 2 fresh limes at whirlpool, added at 170 F for 10 min
- Optional post-fermentation refinement: 0.5-1.0 oz fresh lime juice at packaging only if the beer finishes too lemon-only

Notes:
- Do not use lime in the main boil.
- Avoid pith and white peel.
- First pass should bias toward yeast-driven lemon with lime support, not equal lemon/lime intensity.

## WATER (RO BUILD)
Target profile (ppm):
- Ca: 55-65
- Mg: 5-8
- Na: 0-10
- Cl: 45-55
- SO4: 65-80
- HCO3: 0-20

For total brewing liquor ~8.6 gal (32.6 L), starting from RO:
- Gypsum (CaSO4): 0.10 oz (2.8 g)
- Calcium Chloride (CaCl2): 0.10 oz (2.8 g)
- Epsom Salt (MgSO4): 0.05 oz (1.4 g)
- Baking Soda: 0.00 oz

## VOLUME PLAN (G40 CALIBRATED)
- Total liquor target: ~8.6 gal (32.6 L)
- Pre-boil target: ~7.9 gal (29.9 L)
- Post-boil target (pre-chill): ~5.3 gal (20.1 L)

## MASH PROGRAM
1. Mash at 149.0F (65.0C) for 60 min
2. Mash out at 168.0F (75.6C) for 10 min

## BOIL
- Duration: 60 min
- Add Cascade first wort charge before runoff begins.
- Add corn sugar with 10 minutes left in the boil.
- Add lime zest only at whirlpool temperature, not during active boil.

## FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. Chill and pitch at 66.0F (18.9C).
2. Hold 66.0F for days 0-3.
3. Raise to 68.0F for days 4-6.
4. Crash to 42.0F after FG is stable and citrus profile is clean.

## REVIEW QUESTIONS FOR V1
- Does the yeast/Cascade interaction actually produce convincing lemon character?
- Is the lime support obvious enough without reading pithy or fake?
- Does the beer finish crisp, or does it need more bitterness or more sugar dryness?
- Would the next pass benefit from a second hop voice, or is all-Cascade the right base?

## FIRST-PASS RISKS
- Too much lime reads cleaning-product or cocktail, not beer.
- Too much late Cascade pushes grapefruit instead of lemon.
- Too little bitterness makes the beer feel flabby instead of sparkling.
- Too much chloride or residual sweetness pushes it toward soda, not crisp blonde ale.
