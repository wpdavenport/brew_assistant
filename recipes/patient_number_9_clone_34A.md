# Patient Number 9 (90 Minute Clone - Competition Lock)

## INTENT
Refined 90 Minute clone tuned for competition execution. This is a clone-first recipe targeting smooth but firm bitterness, layered kettle-hop character, and clean process with zero oxidation.

## COMPETITION ENTRY
- BJCP Category: 34A Clone Beer
- Declared Commercial Example: Dogfish Head 90 Minute IPA
- Declared Base Style: 22A Double IPA

## TARGET PARAMETERS
- OG: 1.088-1.092
- FG: 1.014-1.018
- ABV: 8.8-9.5%
- IBU: ~90 (firm, smooth)
- Color: 8-11 SRM
- Mash pH (room temp): 5.25-5.35

## FERMENTABLES
- 15.5 lb Pale Malt
- 1.0 lb Thomas Fawcett Amber Malt
- 1.25 lb Corn Sugar (add late boil)
- 0.5 lb Rice Hulls (optional)

## HOPS (TARGET: ~90 IBU)

### Boil / Whirlpool
- 0.75 oz Simcoe (12.9%) - First Wort
- 0.25 oz Warrior (14.2%) - First Wort
- 0.25 oz Warrior (14.2%) - 60 min
- 0.50 oz Amarillo (7.1%) - 45 min
- 0.50 oz Amarillo (7.1%) - 40 min
- 0.50 oz Simcoe (12.9%) - 40 min
- 0.25 oz Warrior (14.2%) - 40 min
- 0.75 oz Amarillo (7.1%) - 20 min
- 0.75 oz Simcoe (12.9%) - 20 min
- 0.25 oz Warrior (14.2%) - 20 min
- 1.50 oz Amarillo (7.1%) - 0 min at 180 F for 20 min
- 1.50 oz Simcoe (12.9%) - 0 min at 180 F for 20 min

### Dry Hop (Clone-safe, not modern overkill)
- 1.0 oz Amarillo
- 1.0 oz Simcoe
- Timing: add when gravity is within 2-4 points of terminal (typically day 5-6), contact 48-72 hours, then crash.

## YEAST
- WLP007 Dry English Ale

## WATER PROFILE (RO SOURCE)
Total brewing liquor for this recipe: 10.4 gal (39.4 L).

Target ions (approx):
- Ca: ~110 ppm
- Mg: ~8 ppm
- Na: ~0 ppm
- SO4: ~225-230 ppm
- Cl: ~50 ppm
- HCO3: ~0 ppm

Salt additions for 10.4 gal total liquor:
- 0.49 oz Gypsum (CaSO4)
- 0.11 oz Epsom Salt (MgSO4)
- 0.14 oz Calcium Chloride (CaCl2)
- 0.00 oz Baking Soda

## BREW DAY PROCESS (HYBRID NO-SPARGE + PRE-BOIL TOP-UP)
1. Measure 9.5 gal (36.0 L) RO liquor for mash-in and hold 0.9 gal (3.4 L) hot RO as pre-boil top-up reserve.
2. Add salts scaled to total liquor (10.4 gal / 39.4 L). If topping up pre-boil, add a proportional salt share to the reserve water.
3. Heat to strike temp and mash in.
4. Mash 75 minutes at 150 F.
5. Mash out 10 minutes at 168 F.
6. After 10-15 minutes into mash, cool sample and measure pH.
7. Adjust with 85% phosphoric acid only as needed, in 1 ml increments, stir, wait 5 minutes, recheck.
8. Pre-boil targets: 8.5-8.7 gal and 1.056-1.059.
9. Boil 90 minutes and run hop schedule as written.
10. Add 1 Whirlfloc tablet at 15 minutes left in the boil.
11. Add 1.25 lb corn sugar at 10 minutes left in the boil.

## VOLUME CALIBRATION NOTE (G40)
- This version uses calibrated automation-mode boil-off of ~1.76 gal/hr.
- 90 min boil-off expectation: ~2.6 gal.
- Post-boil (pre-chill) target: 5.9-6.1 gal at ~1.081-1.084.

## FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. Chill and pitch at 65 F. Add 2 mL Zinc Buddy directly to fermenter at pitch.
2. Hold 65 F for days 0-3.
3. Raise to 67-68 F for days 4-7 or until terminal gravity.
4. Add dry hops near terminal gravity, hold 48-72 hours at 67-68 F.
5. Cold crash to 38 F immediately after dry hop contact window.

## PACKAGING + CLONE SCORING CHECKPOINTS
- Closed transfer only to fully purged keg.
- Keep oxygen exposure minimal during dry hop and transfer.
- Carbonation target: medium (about 2.3-2.5 vols CO2).
- Validate against fresh commercial sample side-by-side before entry submission.
- Entry form must include exact commercial target + brewery + base style.

## CRITICAL RISKS TO AVOID
- Harshness from excess sulfate or high mash pH.
- Oxidation during dry hop/packaging.
- Over-dry finish that drifts away from clone profile.
- Excess ester/alcohol heat from warm early fermentation.
