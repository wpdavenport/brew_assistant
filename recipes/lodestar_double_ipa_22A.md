# Lodestar

## INTENT
Standalone double IPA spun out of the `PN9` chassis after a process deviation produced a softer, more orange-forward, non-hazy beer that still drank well enough to deserve its own lane.

This is not documented as a clone.

## COMPETITION ENTRY
- BJCP Category: 22A Double IPA
- Concept: orange-forward, non-hazy double IPA with a softer bitter structure
- Direct clone claim: no

## SOURCE BASELINE
- Built directly from `Patient Number 9` V2 recipe structure
- Intentional change for this version: move the 0.25 oz Warrior first-wort addition to the mash

## TARGET PARAMETERS
- OG: 1.090
- FG: 1.016
- ABV: 9.5%
- IBU: ~90 theoretical, but expected to drink softer than `PN9`
- Color: 8 SRM
- Mash pH (room temp): 5.25-5.35

## FERMENTABLES
- 16.0 lb (7.26 kg) Pale Malt
- 0.5 lb (0.23 kg) Thomas Fawcett Amber Malt
- 1.25 lb (0.57 kg) Corn Sugar
- 0.5 lb (0.23 kg) Rice Hulls

Notes:
- Add corn sugar at 10 minutes left in the boil.
- Rice hulls remain optional lauter insurance.

## HOPS

Target: same core `PN9` hop family, but with a softer, rounder expression.

### Mash / Boil / Whirlpool
- 0.25 oz (7 g) Warrior (14.2%) - Mash Hop
- 0.75 oz (21 g) Simcoe (12.9%) - First Wort
- 0.25 oz (7 g) Warrior (14.2%) - 60 min
- 0.50 oz (14 g) Amarillo (7.1%) - 45 min
- 0.50 oz (14 g) Amarillo (7.1%) - 40 min
- 0.50 oz (14 g) Simcoe (12.9%) - 40 min
- 0.75 oz (21 g) Amarillo (7.1%) - 20 min
- 0.75 oz (21 g) Simcoe (12.9%) - 20 min
- 1.50 oz (43 g) Amarillo (7.1%) - 0 min at 180 F for 20 min
- 1.50 oz (43 g) Simcoe (12.9%) - 0 min at 180 F for 20 min

### Dry Hop
- 1.0 oz (28 g) Amarillo
- 1.0 oz (28 g) Simcoe
- Timing: add when gravity is within 2-4 points of terminal, contact 48-72 hours, then crash.

## YEAST
- WLP007 Dry English Ale

- Ca: ~110 ppm
- Mg: ~8 ppm
- Na: ~0 ppm
- SO4: ~225-230 ppm

Salt additions for 10.4 gal total liquor:

- 0.14 oz Calcium Chloride (CaCl2)
- 0.00 oz Baking Soda

## BREW DAY PROCESS (HYBRID NO-SPARGE + PRE-BOIL TOP-UP)
1. Measure 9.5 gal (36.0 L) RO liquor for mash-in and hold 0.9 gal (3.4 L) hot RO as pre-boil top-up reserve.
2. Add salts scaled to total liquor (10.4 gal / 39.4 L). If topping up pre-boil, add a proportional salt share to the reserve water.
3. Heat to strike temp and mash in.
4. Add the 0.25 oz Warrior mash-hop charge during mash-in.
5. Mash 75 minutes at 150 F.
6. Mash out 10 minutes at 168 F.
7. After 10-15 minutes into mash, cool sample and measure pH.
8. Adjust with 85% phosphoric acid only as needed, in 1 ml increments, stir, wait 5 minutes, recheck.
9. Pre-boil targets: 8.5-8.7 gal and 1.056-1.059.
10. Add the Simcoe first-wort charge to the kettle as runoff begins.
11. Boil 90 minutes and run hop schedule as written.
12. Add 1 Whirlfloc tablet at 15 minutes left in the boil.
13. Add 1.25 lb corn sugar at 10 minutes left in the boil.

## VOLUME CALIBRATION NOTE (G40)
- This version uses calibrated automation-mode boil-off of ~1.76 gal/hr.
- 90 min boil-off expectation: ~2.6 gal.
- Post-boil (pre-chill) target: 5.9-6.1 gal at ~1.081-1.084.

## FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. Chill and pitch at 65 F. Add 2 mL Zinc Buddy directly to fermenter at pitch.
2. Hold 65 F for days 0-3.
3. Raise to 67-68 F for days 4-7 or until terminal gravity.
4. Add dry hops near terminal gravity, hold 48-72 hours at 67-68 F.
5. Cold crash to 42 F immediately after dry hop contact window.

## EXPECTED PROFILE
- Orange-forward and highly drinkable
- Softer bitterness than `PN9`
- Non-hazy but still juicy in impression
- Less resinous spine than the strict 90 Minute clone path

## CRITICAL RISKS TO AVOID
- Confusing mash hops with first-wort hops elsewhere in the schedule; only the first 0.25 oz Warrior charge moves into the mash.
- Oxidation during dry hop or packaging.
- Over-dry finish that strips the orange-forward character.
- Running the G40 mash pump wide open and compacting the bed.
