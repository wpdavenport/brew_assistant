## CONCEPT READ
This recipe reads like:
- a massive imperial brown framework
- built with heavy wheat support for body and texture
- driven upward by extract and sugar
- darkened just enough to push roast/chocolate structure without becoming stout
- hopped firmly enough to keep the finish from collapsing

## WHY IT SCORES
- The beer is big, but not random.
- Warrior sets a real bitterness spine under a huge malt bill.
- Palisade and Glacier are unusual but can add a woody/spicy/herbal top layer that suits the concept better than louder citrus-forward hops would.
- 1728 is a credible yeast choice for a strong dark beer when clean malt emphasis is desired.
- The formulation is memorable without needing to pretend it is a straight clone.

## CONSISTENCY RISKS
- Very high OG means batch-to-batch variation can be severe if post-boil volume shifts.
- 3 lb of DME and 1.25 lb of sugar make the beer less dependent on mash efficiency than it looks, but the all-grain portion is still large enough that runoff and boil concentration matter a lot.
- The beer is big enough that weak oxygenation or underpitching can easily produce a hotter, sweeter result.
- Brown sugar can vary in character and moisture content enough to affect repeatability.
- Late hop lots matter more than they appear to because the beer needs just enough lift to avoid reading as syrup-dark.
- If the base-beer identity is not clearly described on the entry form, judges may not know how to reward it.

## STRAIGHT ASSESSMENT
This is not a subtle recipe.
It is probably more process-sensitive than it first appears because:
- gravity is huge
- multiple fermentability levers are in play
- the beer needs enough bitterness and yeast performance to stay integrated

But it also makes sense as an award-winning `34B` beer:
- it is conceptually clear
- it has enough intensity to stand out
- it can be harmonious if fermentation and finishing are controlled

## MOST LIKELY CONSISTENCY LEVERS
If your goal is better repeatability without changing the concept, I would watch these first:
- actual post-boil volume and measured OG every batch
- yeast pitch size and oxygenation consistency
- whether the brown sugar source changed
- actual hop lot AA and freshness on Warrior, Palisade, and Glacier
- whether the beer finished at the same gravity band each time
