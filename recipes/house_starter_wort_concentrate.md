# House Starter Wort Concentrate (12 oz Jar System)

## INTENT
Pressure-can a compact all-grain starter-wort concentrate that fits in `12 oz` mason jars and dilutes with RO water to produce `1.0 L` of starter wort at roughly `1.040`.

This is a house process recipe, not a beer recipe.

## TARGET PARAMETERS
- Jar size: 12 oz mason jars
- Fill per jar: 10 oz concentrate
- Batch size: 8 jars
- Concentrate gravity: 1.135
- Reconstituted starter: 1.0 L at ~1.040
- Dilution per jar: add 23.8 oz (704 mL) RO water
- Hops / bitterness: none

## FERMENTABLES
- 3.40 lb (1.54 kg) Pale Malt
- 0.10 lb (45 g) Light Munich Malt (optional; omit for a cleaner pale wort)

## HOPS

### Kettle Additions
- No hops

## YEAST
- None in the canned concentrate
- Optional at starter time: a small dose of standard beer yeast nutrient

## MASH SCHEDULE
1. Mash at 149.0-150.0F (65.0-65.6C) for 60 minutes.
2. Mash out at 168.0F (75.6C) for 10 minutes.

## BREW DAY PROCESS
1. Collect roughly 1.5-1.8 gal (5.7-6.8 L) of wort.
2. Boil hard with no hops until total kettle volume is reduced to 80 oz.
3. Cool a sample and verify concentrate gravity at about 1.135.
4. If gravity is low, boil longer. If gravity is high, dilute slightly with boiled water.
5. Fill each 12 oz jar with 10 oz of concentrate, leaving headspace.
6. Seal and pressure can in a true pressure canner according to the canner manufacturer's guidance.

## FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. For use, pour 1 jar into a sanitized flask.
2. Add 23.8 oz (704 mL) boiled-and-cooled RO water.
3. Optional: add a small dose of standard beer yeast nutrient.
4. Aerate well, then pitch yeast.
5. Use the resulting 1.0 L starter wort at roughly 1.040 for vitality starters or standard stirred starters.

## PACKAGING + STORAGE
- Store sealed jars in a cool, dark place after pressure canning.
- Label each jar with canning date and target dilution.
- Shake jar before opening if any concentrate settles.
- Best practice: use boiled-and-cooled RO water for reconstitution.

## CRITICAL RISKS TO AVOID
- Do not water-bath can; use a real pressure canner only.
- Do not overfill the jars; preserve headspace.
- Do not guess at final gravity; verify the concentrate before jarring.
- Do not add hops.
- Do not assume raw tap water is clean enough for reconstitution.
