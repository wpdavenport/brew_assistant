# Copper Crown ESB — Iteration Suggestions

## Base Recipe
Refer to: recipes/copper_crown_esb_11C.md (Batch 1 locked recipe)

## Review After Batch 1
Taste critically at 3-6 weeks post-package. Score against the diagnostic tasting grid in libraries/beer_research/11C_strong_bitter.md before making any changes.

Answer these before choosing an iteration path:
- Is the malt depth sufficient, or does it read flat/one-dimensional?
- Is there dark fruit / marmalade complexity, or just clean toffee?
- Is the finish dry-balanced, or does it cling?
- Is bitterness smooth and integrated, or sharp/mineral?
- Are esters present and clean (orchard fruit), or too low / too high?
- Any process faults (diacetyl, oxidation, astringency)?

---

## Iteration A: Dark Fruit / Marmalade Depth (Malt Complexity)
**Trigger:** Batch 1 tastes clean and balanced but lacks the layered dark fruit / marmalade jam character of Fuller's.

**Change:** Split crystal malt — reduce medium crystal, add English dark crystal.
- Medium Crystal (TF): 0.44 lb (0.20 kg) — down from 0.62 lb (4% of grist, down from 6%)
- English Dark Crystal (~75-80L): 0.22 lb (0.10 kg) — new addition (2% of grist)
- Total crystal: 6% (unchanged)

**Expected effect:** Adds dried fruit, raisin, and marmalade notes without increasing sweetness or total crystal load. SRM shifts from ~11 to ~12-13. FG impact: negligible.

**Risk:** Overdone dark crystal can push toward plum/prune and read "old ale" to judges. Keep at 2% max for first test.

**Hold everything else constant.** Same hops, same water, same yeast, same fermentation schedule.

---

## Iteration B: Finish Dryness (If Batch 1 FG lands high)
**Trigger:** Batch 1 FG lands at 1.016+ and finish reads heavy or sweet.

**Change:** Increase Invert Sugar No.2 from 3% to 5%.
- Invert Sugar No.2: 0.53 lb (0.24 kg) — up from 0.35 lb (0.16 kg)
- Reduce Maris Otter by the same weight to hold OG

**Expected effect:** More fermentable wort, lower FG, drier finish, slightly more treacle character. Should drop FG by 1-2 points.

**Risk:** Body thins slightly. If combined with Iteration A, monitor that the beer doesn't read thin + fruity (that drifts toward 12C English IPA territory).

---

## Iteration C: Mash Temperature (If Batch 1 attenuation disappoints)
**Trigger:** Batch 1 FG lands at 1.016+ despite healthy fermentation and proper rouse.

**Change:** Drop mash rest from 151.0 F (66.1 C) to 149.0 F (65.0 C) for 60 min.

**Expected effect:** More fermentable wort, improved attenuation with 1968. Should help FG reach 1.013-1.014.

**Risk:** Below 148 F (64.4 C) risks a thin, dry beer that loses the malt center 11C requires. Don't go below 149 F (65.0 C) without evidence.

---

## Iteration D: Bitterness Perception (If Batch 1 reads sharp or mineral)
**Trigger:** Finish has a harsh or mineral edge despite clean water build.

**Change:** Drop SO4 from 135 to 115 ppm. Raise Cl from 85 to 90 ppm. Ratio moves from ~1.6:1 to ~1.3:1.

**Adjusted salts (for 8.45 gal / 32.0 L):**
- Gypsum: ~5.0 g
- Calcium Chloride: ~6.1 g
- Epsom Salt: ~1.1 g

**Expected effect:** Softer, rounder bitterness perception. More malt-forward balance.

**Risk:** Too much chloride-shift can make the beer read soft and lose the firm bitter snap 11C demands. Don't go below 1.2:1 for this style.

---

## Combining Iterations
- A + B is safe (crystal split + more sugar). Test together if Batch 1 needs both depth and dryness.
- A + C is safe (crystal split + lower mash temp). Good if the issue is both malt complexity and attenuation.
- Do NOT combine B + C in a single jump — both target FG, and stacking them risks overshooting into a thin, dry beer.
- D should be tested alone. Water changes affect everything.

## Ingredient Note
English dark crystal (~75-80L) is already on hand per brewer inventory.

## Rule
One to two controlled changes per batch. Log everything. Compare to Batch 1 notes.
