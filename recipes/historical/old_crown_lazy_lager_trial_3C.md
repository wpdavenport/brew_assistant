# Old Crown Lazy Lager (Historical Trial)

## INTENT
Archive the as-brewed trial version of `Old Crown Lazy Lager` as a hometown-honoring Czech Premium Pale Lager.

This recipe was built to reflect:
- Old Crown Brewing in Fort Wayne, Indiana
- their Czech pilsner
- the brewer's own research and interpretation

This is not documented as a direct clone.

## COMPETITION ENTRY
- BJCP Category: 3C Czech Premium Pale Lager
- Concept: hometown-inspired Czech pilsner
- Direct clone claim: no

## HISTORICAL BREW SETTINGS
- Boil: 90 min
- Batch size target: 6.5 gal
- Mash efficiency target: 80%
- Equipment note from source: G40

## RECIPE AS SUPPLIED

### Fermentables
- 10.00 lb (4.54 kg) Bohemian Pilsner Malt
- 0.75 lb (0.34 kg) Munich II

### Hops
- 1.60 oz (45.4 g) Saaz - first wort
- 1.60 oz (45.4 g) Saaz - 20 min
- 1.60 oz (45.4 g) Saaz - hop stand 10 min at 180.0F

### Kettle Additions
- Whirlfloc at 15 min

### Water Salts
- Gypsum: 0.555 oz
- Epsom: 0.059 oz
- Calcium Chloride: 0.180 oz
- Baking Soda: 0.039 oz

### Yeast
- Jasper Yeast `JY001 - Czech Lager 1`
- Brewer note: Jasper supplied 2 packets sized specifically for this batch, with enough total yeast to skip a starter

## HISTORICAL MASH SCHEDULE
1. Protein rest: 122.0F for 30 min
2. Rest: 148.0F for 5 min
3. Mash: 161.0F for 20 min
4. Rest: 170.0F for 15 min

## HISTORICAL FERMENTATION SCHEDULE
1. 48.0F for 12 days
2. 48.0-52.0F ramp for 1 day
3. 52.0-57.0F ramp for 1 day
4. 57.0-60.0F ramp for 1 day
5. 60.0F for 2 days
6. 60.0-55.0F ramp for 1 day
7. 55.0-50.0F ramp for 1 day
8. 50.0-45.0F ramp for 1 day
9. 42.0F for 21 days

Oxygen notes:
- Day 1: 30 sec oxygen
- Day 3: 30 sec oxygen

## MEASURED BREW NOTES
- Mash-in water: about 9.55 gal
- Post-mash volume: 8.4 gal
- Pre-boil gravity: 1.038
- No pH adjustments other than 4 g of ascorbic acid
- OG: 1.052
- FG: 1.011
- Final ABV: 5.4%

## STYLE FIT
Measured batch result:
- OG 1.052
- FG 1.011
- ABV 5.4%

That sits cleanly in `3C` and reads like a legitimate competition-strength Czech Premium Pale Lager.

## WATER READ
The salt bill is the most aggressive part of the recipe.

If interpreted against the recorded ~9.55 gal mash-in volume, the approximate profile is:
- Ca: ~140 ppm
- Mg: ~5 ppm
- Na: ~8 ppm
- Cl: ~68 ppm
- SO4: ~261 ppm
- HCO3: ~22 ppm

Straight read:
- that is far more sulfate-forward than textbook Czech doctrine
- on paper it should risk harder bitterness than classic Czech targets usually allow
- yet the measured beer still landed in a plausible 3C range, which suggests the brewery inspiration may have leaned firmer than an idealized soft-water Czech pils

## WHY THE RECIPE WORKS
- The grist is restrained and style-faithful.
- Small Munich support is enough to deepen the pils malt without muddying the beer.
- All-Saaz hopping keeps the identity coherent.
- The fermentation schedule is patient and disciplined enough to support polished lager character.
- The measured FG of 1.011 indicates the beer finished crisp enough to stay lively.

## STRAIGHT ASSESSMENT
This recipe holds up.
The recipe logic is cleaner than a lot of homebrew Czech pilsners because it does not overcomplicate the grist or hop bill.

The place I would challenge is water:
- the as-recorded mineral load does not read like classic Czech softness
- if the beer tasted right anyway, that may reflect the target brewery's own expression more than textbook 3C orthodoxy
- if you revisit this beer now, water is the first lever I would reassess before rewriting the malt or hop bill

## CONSISTENCY LEVERS
If you want more repeatability on the next version, watch:
- actual hop lot freshness and alpha acid on Saaz
- the real meaning of the salt bill: total liquor vs mash liquor vs other interpretation
- whether the 2-pack Jasper pitch is still reproducible
- pH measurement, since this batch did not use conventional mash acid correction
- whether your updated crush and mash process change attenuation relative to the historical 1.011 finish
